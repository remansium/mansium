The Estate

Where the Pacific
meets pure architecture

Perched on a dramatic Malibu bluff with unobstructed views from Point Dume to Catalina Island, Casa Lumina is more than a residence — it is a choreography of light, materiality, and stillness. Conceived by Pritzker laureate Marcus Vorel and completed in 2021, this 8,200-square-foot masterwork articulates a singular vision: that a home should feel like a permanent dawn.

Floor-to-ceiling glazing dissolves the boundary between interior and horizon. Walls of white Venetian plaster absorb morning light and release it slowly into the evening. The architecture never competes with its setting — it collaborates.

Location

Provenance

The hand
behind the vision

Marcus Vorel
Principal Architect, Vorel & Associates

Marcus Vorel's practice is built on a single discipline: the precise manipulation of natural light as the primary building material. Working from his studios in Los Angeles and Zurich, he has completed fewer than 40 residential projects over a 22-year career — a record of deliberate restraint that has earned him both the Pritzker Prize (2019) and the unwavering patronage of the world's most discerning private collectors.
